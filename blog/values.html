My Values and Identity

Personal Anecdote from S Schwarz (my flatmate!)

I believe that one of Max's strengths is her creative mind and willingness to try new things. I first met Max at university and we studied Japanese together. Our Sensei would set us homework in which we would have to use designated grammar structures in an original sentence of our own. Together to add more depth to the homework we would challenge ourselves to make one story with all of the target structures included. Creativity

Having a creative and individual mind has always been a value I have cherished since I was young. Self-expression has always been a big thing for me, and I was always doodling things and playing music as a child. I think to be able to create something of your own, whether it be in the form of art or a new way of problem solving and thinking, is extremely important. Each individual is unique and has their own way of viewing the world and processing things and it is really important to express this and also be accepting of other people's ways. I think this is something that led me into Web Development as I dabbled in it while I was teaching and had an enormous amount of fun thinking up an idea for a website, problem solving and getting those a-ha! moments when something I had been working on finally clicked together.

“Creativity is inventing, experimenting, growing, taking risks, breaking rules, making mistakes, and having fun.” - Mary Lou Cook

Curiosity

I've always been a curious person, and that has heavily influenced what I do and where I have gone in life. When I find something that piques my interest, I will research and dive deep into things and usually end up wanting to experience it for myself. This is how I found myself moving across the globe and living in Japan for 2 years and Korea for 5. I wanted to know what was beyond the borders and see it myself. I also have many interests and hobbies that I have tried, which has given me a breadth of knowledge which helps me connect with others through common ground. I am also curious about other people and love to hear their story, as I believe everyone has a unique one to tell and deserves to be heard.

"The important thing is not to stop questioning. Curiosity has its own reason for existing." - Albert Einstein

Enthusiasm

When given a task, I find that (ADHD willing) I like to put my all into it and don't try to skate by on the minimum. I love to form plans and ideas with people and like to make sure they feel seen and listened when meeting someone new. I also dive into new ideas with enthusiasm, and like to envision all the possibilities with optimism. I also think this is important when working with other people, as others can sense a lack of enthusiasm and can be negatively affected by it, but positively effects by seeing someone else's excitement. I learned this quickly when I was a teacher, as bringing my own enthusiastic energy into the classroom would in turn cause students to become enthusiastic about the topic and their learning.

"I believe that education is all about being excited about something. Seeing passion and enthusiasm helps push an educational message." - Steve Irwin

Compassion

I believe that I while compassion has always been a strong value for me, I really learned the importance of it while I lived overseas in Asia. Not only did people show a lot of compassion for me and often helped me out when I was struggling because of language or cultural walls, I also made an effort to show it every day in my work. I was an EFL teacher and taught all ages, and I always tried my absolute best to communicate with my students with the compassion that was shown to me. They all had varying levels of skill, and I made sure that they all felt at ease when speaking to me and I would care for them as individuals as well. For example, I would talk to students who were worried about their grades or daily life and do my best to listen and give advice not just about English, but talk about what it means to learn, keep trying and follow your passions. All of my students were people on journeys just like I am. I believe that if we all show compassion and care towards each other, strangers and closest friends alike, we will lift each other up and create a good atmosphere where people feel safe and supported.

“There is no small act of kindness. Every compassionate act makes large the world.” – Mary Anne Radmacher

Work

An ethical decision I have made was to talkto my boss about the breach of Korean Labor Standards that I found while I was teaching English in Korea. I had seen only that this was the case for many places but not often talked about. Because it piqued my curiosity I ended up doing hours and hours of research. I found that my employer, along with the majority of academies in Korea, were giving 1-7 less days worth of paid leave than was legally allowed. I found this out at the very end of my contract.

I could have slipped away quietly and done nothing, with a guarantee that my end-of- contract bonus and final pay were not at risk. However I realised my co-workers who were staying would not recieve the benefits if I sat and did nothing, and they were still contracted for at least 6 more months so I decided to gather my evidence and confront our boss myself. I had come to feel very strongly about it as it was unjust that these decade old laws were being ignored and employees were not receiving their due rest. With the laws printed out I plucked up the courage to ask to meet with my boss, and presented my case. I understood that it was not out of malice but out of a lack of knowledge, and because of my non-confrontational manner it went well.

She called her boss, and then the next day she called me in for another meeting. She let me know that not only would they be changing my contract to include my extra day, but every single other employee in the 200+ person business would have their contracts updated to fit the law, and compensated for fairly. I was ecstatic to find out what a change I had made due to me keeping to values of compassion, enthusiasm and curiosity.

One time I have tried to work productively with others but experienced tension was when my coworker and I were in charge of creating a curriculum for classes but were tasked with our own books to work on with no collaboration. We settled on themes together but after that had little communication between what was done. We worked at different speeds, and the books ended up with some major design and content differences between them, and there was frustration because of that. Knowing what I know now, I think it would have been better to work on the books all together in a shared document, with each person working to their strengths- eg, someone gathering the content, someone putting it together to design the pages, someone editing and then someone taking those and creating PPTs from them.
